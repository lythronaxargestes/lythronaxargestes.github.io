About

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in the Societal Computing program of the Software & Societal Systems Department (S3D) of the School of Computer Science (SCS) at CMU. My research interests lie at the intersection of reinforcement learning, human-computer interaction, and computational game theory, with a focus on applications to socially impactful domains such as transportation and digital privacy. My work aims to evaluate the impact of real-world uncertainty and user behaviour on AI algorithms designed for these domains, with the goal of moving them from sanitized simulations into actionable deployments.

My current advisors are Fei Fang and Norman Sadeh. For my undergraduate degree, I was advised at the University of British Columbia by Kevin Leyton-Brown and his PhD students, Chris Cameron and Jason Hartford.
